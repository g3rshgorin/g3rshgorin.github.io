At the core of ecosystems are patterns
in the behaviors and fates of individuals

Collective behavior⏤as seen in flocks, herds and crowds⏤occurs when individual interactions propegate to affect the behavior of groups. From biofilms to cities, collectives systematically alter the flow of materials, energy and information in ecosystems, in ways that are difficult to predict with current tools. Our research seeks to understand how collectives mediate and respond to ecosystem change, by synthesizing high-throughput observational data, -omics approaches, and mathematical models. We work in a range of contexts, focussed on improving the health of human and animal populations, and enhancing the stability of social-ecological systems under global change.

Infectious disease dynamics
in cities

Understanding of the role of urbanization in the emergence, spread and diversification of infectious pathogens

Collective migration

Investigating the impact of collective behavior on the emergence and stability of migration patterns under global change

Microbial biogeography
in metapopulations of hosts

Exploring how animal movement patterns shape the diversity and stability of microbial communities over space, time and scale
