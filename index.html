At the core of living systems are patterns
in the behaviors and fates of individuals

These patterns underly everything‚è§from the microbes we harbor in our bodies to the global migration routes of animals. The individuals involved in these processes often respond to each other, as seen in biofilms, flocks, herds and crowds. This collective behavior could have profound implications for how living systems function, but it is rarely included in the models we use to understand biological data. Our research seeks a predictive understanding of the role of collectives in ecological and evolutionary dynamics, by synthesizing high-throughput observational data, laboratory approaches, and mathematical models. We work in a range of contexts, focussed on improving the health of human and animal populations, and enhancing the stability of social-ecological systems under global change.
