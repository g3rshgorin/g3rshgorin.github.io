At the core of living systems are patterns
in the behaviors and fates of individuals. These patterns underly everything‚è§from the microbes we harbor in our bodies to the global migration routes of animals.

The individuals involved in theses processes often respond to each other, as seen in flocks, herds and crowds. This could have profound implications for how living systems function, but collective behavior is rarely included in the models we use to understand biological data. Our research seeks a predictive understanding of the role of collectives in ecological and evolutionary dynamics, by synthesizing high-throughput observational data, laboratory approaches, and mathematical models. We work in a range of contexts, including the ecology and evolution of infectious disease Our focus is on improving the health of human and animal populations, and enhancing the stability of social-ecological systems under global change.
